The closest planet to the sun, Mercury is a rocky planet with a surface covered in craters. It has a diameter of about 4,880 kilometers and is the smallest planet in the solar system. It has a very thin atmosphere and no moons.

The second planet from the sun, Venus is a rocky planet with a thick, toxic atmosphere. It is often referred to as Earth's sister planet because it is similar in size and mass. Venus has a diameter of about 12,104 kilometers and no moons.

The third planet from the sun, Earth is a rocky planet with a diverse range of environments and habitats. It has a diameter of about 12,742 kilometers and one moon. Earth is the only planet in the solar system known to support life.

The fourth planet from the sun, Mars is a rocky planet with a thin atmosphere. It has a diameter of about 6,792 kilometers and two moons. Mars is known for its reddish appearance and is often referred to as the "Red Planet."

The fifth planet from the sun, Jupiter is a gas giant with a diameter of about 142,984 kilometers. It is the largest planet in the solar system and has 79 known moons. Jupiter is known for its distinctive red spot, which is a giant storm.

The sixth planet from the sun, Saturn is a gas giant with a diameter of about 120,536 kilometers. It has 53 known moons and is known for its beautiful ring system.

The seventh planet from the sun, Uranus is a gas giant with a diameter of about 51,118 kilometers. It has 27 known moons and is known for its unusual axial tilt, which causes it to rotate on its side.

The eighth planet from the sun, Neptune is a gas giant with a diameter of about 49,528 kilometers. It has 14 known moons and is known for its blue appearance.
